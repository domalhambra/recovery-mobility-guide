# Code of Conduct

## Purpose and Values
Our goal is to foster an inclusive, professional, and productive environment where every individual is treated with empathy and respect. We prioritize a "people first" culture, ensuring that all members feel safe and valued regardless of their background, identity, or position. This code outlines the standards for our interactions and the mechanisms for addressing behavior that falls short of these values.

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and partners. It covers interactions in the following contexts:
* Physical office spaces and remote work environments.
* Digital communication channels (email, Slack, social media).
* Work-related social functions, conferences, and industry events.
* Any situation where an individual is representing the company.

## Expected Behavior
* **Inclusive Collaboration:** Seek out diverse perspectives and acknowledge the contributions of others.
* **Respectful Communication:** Practice active listening and use professional language.
* **Accountability:** Take responsibility for your actions and their impact on the team.
* **Ethical Conduct:** Handle company property and data with integrity and confidentiality.

## Unacceptable Behavior
The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:
* **Harassment and Discrimination:** Any verbal, physical, or written abuse based on race, color, religion, sex, age, disability, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other protected characteristic.
* **Bullying and Intimidation:** Persistent demeaning conduct, threats, or victimization.
* **Subtle Bias:** Belittling comments or behavior that reinforces harmful stereotypes.
* **Inappropriate Physical Contact:** Any unwelcome physical contact or sexual advances.
* **Intoxication:** Excessive consumption of alcohol or use of illegal substances during work hours or at work events.
* **Retaliation:** Treating anyone poorly for reporting a concern or participating in an investigation.

## Reporting Procedures
If you experience or witness a violation of this code, use one of the following channels:
1.  **Direct Manager:** Contact your supervisor unless they are involved in the incident.
2.  **Human Resources:** Email [Insert HR Email] for a formal review.
3.  **Anonymous Reporting:** Use the [Insert Tool Name/Link] to submit a report without identifying yourself.

**What to Expect After Reporting:**
* **Receipt:** You will receive a confirmation within 24 hours.
* **Investigation:** A confidential review will begin immediately, involving relevant parties while protecting the reporter's identity to the fullest extent possible.
* **Timeline:** Most investigations are resolved within [10] business days.
* **Resolution:** You will be notified when the matter is closed, and managers will follow up at three and six months to ensure the behavior has stopped.

## Enforcement
Violations of this code will result in disciplinary action tailored to the severity of the incident. Consequences may include:
* Verbal or written warnings.
* Mandatory training or coaching.
* Suspension or shift in reporting structure.
* Termination of employment or contract.

## Offsite and Social Events
Our standards remain in effect at all company-sponsored social events.
* **Alcohol:** If alcohol is served, non-alcoholic options must be equally available. Intoxication is not an excuse for misconduct.
* **Inclusivity:** Events should be held at accessible venues and at times that accommodate those with caregiving responsibilities.
* **Invitations:** All official and unofficial work outings should be shared publicly (e.g., on a shared Slack channel) to avoid exclusionary practices.

## Romantic Relationships
To maintain a professional environment and manage power dynamics:
* **Disclosure:** Employees must disclose any romantic or sexual relationship with a colleague to HR within 24 hours of the first date.
* **Reporting Lines:** Employees may not have a direct reporting relationship with someone they are dating. If such a relationship develops, the company will alter the chain of command.
* **Solicitation:** We follow a "one-shot" policy; you may ask a coworker out once, but if the answer is no, you must not ask again.
